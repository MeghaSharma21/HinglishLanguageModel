 nirdeshaank: 31°03′35″N 76°18′10″E / 31.0596618°N 76.3028812°E / 31.0596618; 76.3028812balachaur uttar bharat ke punjab rajya me navanshahar shahar kii ek tehsil he. bhalaa chaur doaba kshetra ke andar aata he or kaafii saare gaanv iske andar aate he. bhalaa chaur main choudhary rahamat ali kaa jnm hua tha.
 kushwaha rajput raaj dev ne 16veen shatabdi me pehali baar bhalaa chaur me aakar rahanaa shuru kiya apane parivaar ke saath, unka sambadh jaipur ke raaj gharaane ke saath tha. raaj dev ne yaha bhakthi kerani shuru kii or is jagah kaa naam apane putr balraj dev ke naam par rakhaa. raaj dev kii mrityu 1596 me hui. sthania logo ne unki mazaar banaai or use baba balraj ke naam se poojane lage. aaj bhi baba balraj kaa mandir bhalaa chaur me sthit he, mandir kii dekh rekh ke liye mandir cameti bhi banaai gayi 1949 me jinke sangrakshak balavant singh the. 1539 me shershah suri ne humayun par hamalaa karne se pehle yahi par baba raaj dev se ashish lee thi.
 2001 kii janaganana ke anusaar bhalaa chaur kii jansankhya 19106 thi, jisame 53% purush or 47% mahilaaye he. sakshartaa kii dar 68% he jo kii rashtria dar se upar he. 56% purush or 44% mahilaayein sakshar he. 12% jansankhya 6 varsh se kam aayu kii he. tehsil hone ke kaaran bhalaa chaur, aas paas ke gramin kshetro ke liye kaafii mahatv purna he. yaha par kaafii sarkari daphtar he, sthania adaalat bhi yahi he or kaafii bada bazaar bhi he. 
 kuch pramukh gaanv jo tehsil ke andar aate he unamem rateval, thopia, katwaraa, takerala, chaniyani aadi.
 bhalaa chaur me koi bada udyog nahi he, yaha or aas paas ke logo kii aay kaa mukhya strot kheti badi hi he. genhu or makka pramukh fasale he jo kii saal me ugaai jaati he. shikshit varg sampoornataya naukari karnaa pasand karata he. kaafii log aas paas ke shahro me hi naukari karte he. kisaan varg apni fasal bhalaa chaur kii anaaj mandi me laakar bechate he. bhalaa chaur me ilake kaa sabase bada bazaar he jahaan zarurat kii sabhi chize mil jaati he. bhalaa chaur me ke si cenima ghar bhi he jahaan nayi filme dikhaai jaati he.
